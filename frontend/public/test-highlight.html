Feature: URL-based Passage Highlighting

This demo shows how passage links work in the Verse application.

Example URL:

http://localhost:5173?book=John&chapter=3&verse=16

Visual Result:

14 As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up,
15 that whoever believes in him may have eternal life.
16 For God so loved the world, that he gave his only born Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish, but have eternal life.
17 For God didn't send his Son into the world to judge the world, but that the world should be saved through him.
18 He who believes in him is not judged. He who doesn't believe has been judged already, because he hasn't believed in the name of the only born Son of God.

Verse Range Highlighting

You can also highlight a range of verses:

Example URL:

http://localhost:5173?book=John&chapter=3&verseStart=16&verseEnd=17

Visual Result:

15 that whoever believes in him may have eternal life.
16 For God so loved the world, that he gave his only born Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish, but have eternal life.
17 For God didn't send his Son into the world to judge the world, but that the world should be saved through him.
18 He who believes in him is not judged. He who doesn't believe has been judged already, because he hasn't believed in the name of the only born Son of God.

URL Parameters Supported

  • book - Bible book name (e.g., "John", "1 Corinthians")
  • chapter - Chapter number (required)
  • verse - Single verse to highlight
  • verseStart - Start of verse range
  • verseEnd - End of verse range
  • translation - Bible translation code (e.g., "KJV", "WEB")

How It Works:

  1. When a user clicks a passage link, the URL parameters are parsed
  2. The entire chapter is loaded for context
  3. The specified verse(s) are highlighted with a yellow background
  4. The page smoothly scrolls to bring the highlighted verse into view
  5. The PassageSearch component works the same way - searching for a verse highlights it
